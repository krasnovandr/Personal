On December 13, 2010, the band announced it would again play as part of the &#34;big four&#34; during the Sonisphere Festival at Knebworth House, Hertfordshire, on July 8, 2011. It was the first time all of the &#34;big four&#34; members played on the same stage in the UK.[101] On December 17, 2010, Another &#34;big four&#34; Sonisphere performance that would take place in France on July 9 was announced.[102] On January 25, 2011, another &#34;big four&#34; performance on April 23, 2011, at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California, was announced. It was the first time all of the &#34;big four&#34; members played on the same stage in the U.S.[103] On February 17, 2011, a show in Gelsenkirchen, Germany, on July 2, 2011, was announced.[104] On February 22, a &#34;big four&#34; show in Milan on July 6, 2011, was announced.[105] On March 2, 2011, another &#34;big four&#34; concert, which took place in Gothenburg on July 3, 2011, was announced.[106] The final &#34;big four&#34; concert was in New York City, at Yankee Stadium, on September 14, 2011.[107]On June 15, 2011, Metallica announced that recording sessions with singer-songwriter Lou Reed had concluded. The album, which was titled Lulu, was recorded over several months and comprised ten songs[108] based on Frank Wedekind's &#34;Lulu&#34; plays Earth Spirit and Pandora's Box.[109] The album was released on October 31, 2011. The recording of the album was problematic at times; Lars Ulrich later said Lou Reed challenged him to a &#34;street fight&#34;.[110] On October 16, 2011, Robert Trujillo confirmed that the band was back in the studio and writing new material. He said, &#34;The writing process for the new Metallica album has begun. We've been in the studio with Rick Rubin, working on a couple of things, and we're going to be recording during the most of next year.&#34;[111]
