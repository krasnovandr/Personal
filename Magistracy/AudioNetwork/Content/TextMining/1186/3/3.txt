Metallica's third studio album, Master of Puppets, was recorded at Sweet Silence Studios and was released in March 1986. The album reached number 29 on the Billboard 200and spent 72 weeks on the chart.[26] It was the band's first album to be certified gold on November 4, 1986, and was certified six times platinum in 2003.[27] Steve Huey of AllMusic considered the album &#34;the band's greatest achievement&#34;.[28] Following the release of the album, Metallica supported Ozzy Osbourne on a U.S. tour.[24] Hetfield broke his wrist while skateboarding; he continued with the tour, performing vocals, with guitar technician John Marshall playing rhythm guitar.[29]On September 27, 1986, during the European leg of Metallica's Damage, Inc.
